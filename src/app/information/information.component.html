This study is about the perceptions and behaviors of researchers, professionals and students in software engineering area related to soft skills in their experiences.
Software Engineering (SE) is an area that requires several logical abilities (Hard Skills), and, in this way, those kinds of skills were considered the most important in the past. However, nowadays, subjective qualities, as known as Soft Skills (SSs) are as important as the logical ones, because professionals must have the capacity to learn, working in teams, oral and writing skills, or even wellness in a job once the software development is a result of human activities, which often include problem-solving capabilities, cognitive aspects, and social interaction

What are Soft Skills?
Soft Skills are the skills related to human personalities and behaviors rather than technical aptitude or knowledge. For instance, they are skills that determine one's strengths as a leader, facilitator or negociator.
Some Soft Skills:
1. Communication
2. Self-taught
3. Organization

What are Hard Skills?
Hard Skill are the countable skills, it is the knowledge about having a solid foundation in mathematics, sciences, engineering and as well as skills in computer science field just like data structures, numerical analysis, data management, network science or programming languages. They are the techinal qualities an individual can have to perform a task or a job, just as programming languages
Some Hard Skills:
1. Java Programming
2. Management skills
3. Adobe XD
4. Basic computer skills (e.g. Email, Spreadsheets)

The importance
The need for Soft Skills has increased along with Software Engineering evolution. In this perspective, those human skills become an important factor in job requirements [1]. As projects, complexity increases over the years, the human interaction as communicate with teammates or even with clients becomes something common and indispensable. Then, the industry does not require only professionals with Hard Skills but also professionals who have well-developed Soft Skills [2].
